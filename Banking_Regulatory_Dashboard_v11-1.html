FDIC — Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation · State Non-Member Banks & Savings Associations

The Regulatory Pendulum:
113 Years of US Banking Regulation

Four visualizations of the same story — the US banking regulatory environment plotted against a zero baseline, where upward movement represents increasing regulatory intensity and downward movement represents deregulation. Each visual frames the same underlying data differently to reveal distinct aspects of the cycle.

The index is a composite Bank Regulation Intensity (BRI) score derived from the Mahmood FDIC 2024 working paper, Dagher IMF 2018 regulatory cycles research, and Federal Register rulemaking counts. Positive values = tighter than average. Negative values = looser than average. The pattern is unmistakable: every deregulatory trough since 1913 has been followed by a crisis. We are currently near the deepest trough since the pre-2008 nadir.
